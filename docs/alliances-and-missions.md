# Alliances & Missions

## Alliances

An alliance is a team of players working together to complete a pixel-art mission on the map.

### Creating an Alliance

When you create an alliance, you define:
- **Name** — Displayed on the leaderboard
- **Description** — What your alliance is about
- **Mission** — A pixel-art image and its position on the map

The creator becomes the **leader**. Leadership cannot be transferred.

### Pricing

Alliance creation uses tiered pricing:

| Alliance # | Cost |
|-----------|------|
| 1st (in the game) | Free |
| 2nd | $3 |
| 3rd | $5 |
| 4th | $7 |
| 5th+ | $10 |

50% goes to the project owner, 40% to the season treasury, 10% to the mission reward pool.

### Joining & Leaving

- Anyone can freely join any alliance
- A player can only be in **one alliance at a time**
- Leaving an alliance has a cooldown period
- The leader cannot leave their own alliance

### Leaderboard

Alliances are ranked by **total pixels captured** — the number of pixels on the map currently owned by alliance members.

## Missions

Every alliance has exactly one mission — a pixel-art template that must be painted onto the map.

### How Missions Work

1. The leader uploads an image and places it at specific coordinates during alliance creation
2. The image is converted to a pixel template matching the game's palette
3. Alliance members (and anyone else) paint pixels to match the template
4. When **95% of pixels match** — the mission is complete

### Mission Completion

When a mission reaches 95%:
- An **NFT** is automatically minted to the alliance leader
- The mission round is closed and rewards become claimable
- The leader can **upgrade** the mission (expand it)

### Upgrading Missions

After completion, the leader can expand the mission:
- The new template must **contain the old area** (can only grow, not shrink)
- A new round begins — only pixels placed after the upgrade count for new rewards
- The old NFT is burned when the upgraded mission completes, and a new (more valuable) NFT is minted

This creates a loop: complete mission, expand, complete again, expand — earning rewards at each step.

### Non-Members Helping

Anyone can place correct pixels in any mission. Non-members receive **half the reward weight** (x1) compared to alliance members (x2). They help the mission but earn less.

### Sabotage

There is no protection against overwriting mission pixels. Other players can sabotage missions by painting over correct pixels. This is by design — defend your territory.
